737 MAX Overview

The Boeing 737 MAX is the fourth generation of the Boeing 737, a narrow-body airliner manufactured by Boeing Commercial Airplanes.

Fuel Efficiency

14% more fuel-efficient than the previous 737NG, thanks to advanced CFM LEAP-1B engines and aerodynamic improvements.

Aerodynamic Design

Features distinctive split-tip winglets that reduce drag and improve fuel efficiency by up to 1.5%.

Advanced Systems

Equipped with the Boeing Advanced Technology winglet and redesigned engine nacelles for optimal performance.

737 MAX Anatomy

Key components and design features of the Boeing 737 MAX aircraft

Key Components Explained

  • CFM LEAP-1B Engines: Larger, more efficient engines mounted higher and further forward on the wing to accommodate their size.
  • Split Scimitar Winglets: Unique wingtip design that reduces vortex drag, improving fuel efficiency and range.
  • Maneuvering Characteristics Augmentation System (MCAS): A flight control system designed to automatically adjust the aircraft's pitch in certain flight conditions.
  • Redesigned Fuselage: Features Boeing Sky Interior with LED lighting, larger overhead bins, and improved cabin pressure.
  • Advanced Avionics: Includes large displays, enhanced navigation systems, and improved flight management capabilities.

The 737 MAX features a distinctive design with its larger engines positioned further forward and higher on the wing compared to previous 737 models. This required aerodynamic modifications including the addition of the MCAS system to address changes in flight characteristics.

Safety Enhancements

Comprehensive safety improvements implemented after the grounding of the 737 MAX fleet

MCAS Redesign

MCAS now relies on input from two angle-of-attack sensors and can only activate once per event. It also compares data from both sensors to ensure validity.

Enhanced Training

Comprehensive pilot training on MCAS functionality, including computer-based training and simulator sessions focused on recognizing and responding to MCAS-related scenarios.

System Redundancy

Additional safeguards including cross-checks between flight control computers and enhanced monitoring systems to prevent erroneous MCAS activation.
